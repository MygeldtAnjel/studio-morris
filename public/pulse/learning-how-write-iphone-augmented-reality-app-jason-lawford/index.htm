I have been in the architectural field now for over 30 years, starting briefly on the drawing board, but soon moving onto Autocad AEC and then onto Archicad, both full 3D architectural modelling packages. In the late 80's it would take 24 hours to do a basic hidden line view of a large building (no shading, just lines), this would then be hand drawn over on tracing paper to produce a representative image that you could share with the client/authority.

In Archicad (in the early 90's) we would painfully produce animations of new large scale developments for Bournemouth University, a local social housing group or local authority. The resulting animations were very rastery and clunky, and would be delivered on video tape with accompanying coloured brochure, as the internet did not exist.

Now, in Archicad, I can produce hugely detailed and high quality models of my designs/projects, not only intelligently outputting the traditional range of planning or construction documentation, but also sharing the 3D model in a range of different ways:

Live Model - Still CGI Views - Animations. The live Archicad model can be easily used during meetings to illustrate topics of discussions. This model can then also be exported into visualisation software to produce realistic still images or animations, in huge quality, and at a speed that would have been totally unbelievable 10+ years ago.

IFC - The 3D model can be shared with all other consultants and brought together into one unified model to ensure that all disciplines proposals work together.

Virtual Reality (VR) - This has become mainstream in the past few years, and comes in a few different flavours (all of which I am able to produce).

VR - Panoramas - This is where 360 degree still images are created that can be used either on a desktop, headset or mobile device, and can be linked together to allow you to navigate a preset path, and see all around each viewpoint, with links to the next viewpoint.

VR - 360˙ Animation - This works on the same devices as the VR Panoramas, and is a preset path, but you float through the fully animated scenes, whilst able to look all around as you pass along the path.

VR - FULL VR - This is where I can provide you with a full VR experience in a (IRO) 6GB file. For this you will need a powerful gaming quality computer and a proper VR headset. In return for this requirement you are able to navigate anywhere in your model, change the time of day (the lights automatically come on when dark), change the season and weather, and even change the materials being used. This is a fully immersive experience that I guarantee will bring a smile to the faces of everybody who uses it.

VR is great for experiencing a design, but it is not best suited to fully understand a design, this is where Augmented Reality (AR) comes in.

AR is great for not only joining together the 2D images and the 3D model, but it is also a great way to bring proposals down to a scale that the viewer can go at their own pace and understand.

AR enables you to bring the traditional architectural model to the views desk or kitchen table, via their mobile device.

The problem is, the path to AR is not easy and very restrictive.

After failing to find a good and reliable solution I decided to create my own, and so for the past few weeks I have been learning how to code an AR iPhone App from scratch, and after much time with a cold flannel on my head I have managed it.
